<p><strong>ZIESTA </strong>is Zhenghua Secondary School’s signature programme
that provides students with authentic learning experiences beyond the classroom.
Anchored in the school’s Applied Learning Programme (ALP) and Learning
for Life Programme (LLP), ZIESTA allows students to apply what they have
learnt in real-world and experiential contexts. Held at the beginning of
Term 2, the programme engages students in a range of carefully designed
activities that develop essential 21st-century competencies while nurturing
confidence, curiosity and resilience. Through ZIESTA, students grow as
Dynamic Learners, Exemplary Leaders and Thoughtful Contributors.
<br>
</p>
<h4><strong><u>Secondary 1</u></strong><br></h4>
<p>The Sec 1 Ziesta programme allows students to cultivate a deeper appreciation
of sustainability, culture, and heritage while developing the capacity
to apply knowledge beyond the classroom as reflective and adaptable Dynamic
Learners. Through the sustainability learning journey to Marina Barrage,
they gain insights into Singapore’s innovative water management and renewable
energy strategies, reinforcing environmental consciousness and responsibility
and applying Mathematics concepts. The cultural learning journeys to Joo
Chiat, Chinatown, and Geylang Serai immerse students in diverse traditions,
food, and architecture, fostering intercultural understanding and social
cohesion. Meanwhile, the heritage learning journey to the Singapore River
enables them to trace the nation’s historical evolution from a trading
port to a modern city-state, strengthening their grasp of historical continuity
and identity. Collectively, these experiences provide authentic contexts
for inquiry, reflection, and collaboration, empowering students to connect
classroom learning with real-world applications.</p>

<h4><strong><u>Secondary 2</u></strong><br></h4>
<p>The Sec 2 Ziesta Programme, All Aboard STEM, provides students with meaningful
opportunities to learn beyond the classroom through three key components:
STEM-in Transport Learning Journeys, Inclusivity Sports, and the Inclusivity
VIA Project.</p>
<p>Through the Inclusivity Sports segment featuring Goalball and Sitting
Volleyball, students gain a deeper appreciation of the challenges faced
by persons with different needs and abilities. They develop empathy, respect,
teamwork, and the importance of supporting others within their means, while
cultivating the qualities of a Thoughtful Contributor.</p>
<p>The STEM-in-Transport learning journeys to organisations such as SMRT,
the Land Transport Authority (LTA), SBS Transit, and the Maritime and Port
Authority of Singapore provide first-hand insights into Singapore’s transport
systems, including their advanced infrastructure and innovative technologies.
These experiences broaden students’ understanding of how STEM is applied
in real-world contexts to address challenges in logistics, sustainability,
efficiency, and safety.</p>
<p>Building on these experiences, students apply their learning through the
Inclusivity VIA Project, where they collaborate in groups to propose solutions
that address the needs of different members of the community. The programme
strengthens students’ understanding of STEM and inclusivity, while encouraging
them to become compassionate, socially responsible, and forward-thinking
individuals.</p>

<h4><strong><u>Secondary 3</u></strong><br></h4>
<p>The Secondary 3 camp is designed to forge strong class spirit, cultivate
resilience, and develop leadership capabilities through immersive, hands-on
challenges. Through physically and mentally engaging activities such as
hiking, rock climbing, tent pitching, and outdoor cooking, students are
encouraged to step beyond their perceived limits and translate the concept
of perseverance into lived experience. These outdoor experiences provide
authentic opportunities for students to grow as Dynamic Learners who adapt
to changing environments, Exemplary Leaders who guide their peers through
shared challenges, and Thoughtful Contributors who support one another
in building a cohesive community. Ultimately, the camp bridges individual
resilience and collective unity, allowing students to return to the classroom
with greater confidence, empathy, and a stronger sense of responsibility
in contributing meaningfully to the school community.</p>

<h4><strong><u>Secondary 4</u></strong><br></h4>
<p>The Education and Career Guidance (ECG) Symposium provides students with
a meaningful platform to explore future education and career pathways.
Designed to broaden perspectives and inspire aspirations, the programme
connects students with higher education representatives and more than 50
speakers from various industries and post-secondary institutions, who share
valuable insights into the professional world and further education opportunities.</p>
<p>A key highlight of the symposium is the mock interview sessions conducted
by our dedicated alumni and parent volunteers. Through these sessions,
students benefit from constructive feedback and practical advice in a supportive
environment. Many students find the authentic interview experience useful
in building their confidence and preparing them for the Early Admissions
Exercise (EAE).</p>

<p>
<br>Building on the learning experiences earlier in the year, ZIESTA also
takes place during the final week of the school year, providing students
with further opportunities to consolidate and extend their learning. This
culminating experience allows students to reflect on their growth, apply
their skills in more complex and integrated tasks, and demonstrate their
readiness to take on future challenges. Together, the two phases of ZIESTA
support holistic development by encouraging students to think flexibly,
lead with purpose, and contribute meaningfully to the community.</p>
