<h4><u>Learning Beyond Borders</u></h4>
<p>Zhenghua Secondary School's annual overseas learning programme, the <strong>Trip for International Experience (TIE)</strong>,
offers students purposeful exposure beyond Singapore. Each TIE trip is
thoughtfully crafted to blend <strong>curriculum learning with cultural immersion</strong>.
This allows students to apply their classroom knowledge in authentic contexts,
thereby fostering global awareness and developing essential 21st Century
Competencies.</p>
<p>Through the overseas experiences, students deepen their understanding
of different cultures, build confidence in engaging with the wider world,
and reflect on Singapore’s role both regionally and globally.</p>

<h4><u>Purposeful Overseas Learning Journeys</u></h4>
<p>Each year, the school offers <strong>three TIE overseas trips</strong>,
planned intentionally to support different learning domains. While all
trips emphasise cultural immersion, each is anchored in <strong>distinct disciplinary learning goals</strong>,
ensuring coherence and progression across the programme.</p>
<p>The three annual TIE trips are:</p>
<p>●&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Brunei</strong> – Cultural
Immersion with <em>Mathematics and English Language</em>
<br>●&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Hanoi, Vietnam</strong> –
Cultural Immersion with <em>Humanities</em>
<br>●&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>Shanghai–Wuxi, China</strong> –
Cultural Immersion with <em>Science, Mother Tongue Language and ALP (STEM)</em>
</p>
<p>Together, these experiences broaden students’ perspectives, strengthen
disciplinary understanding, and nurture values essential for life beyond
the classroom.</p>
<hr>
<h4><u>Brunei: Cultural Immersion with Mathematics and English Language</u></h4>
<p><strong><em>Learning Focus<br></em></strong><em>Applying mathematical reasoning and effective communication in real‑world contexts</em>
</p>
<p>Students experience Brunei through school immersion and cultural activities
that require them to observe, communicate and collaborate in unfamiliar
settings. Learning experiences are designed to develop students’ clarity
of expression, adaptability and teamwork, while strengthening appreciation
of ASEAN cultures and ways of life.</p>

<h4><u>Hanoi, Vietnam: Cultural Immersion with Humanities</u></h4>
<p><strong><em>Learning Focus<br></em></strong><em>Understanding how history, geography and society shape identity and lived experiences</em>
</p>
<p>The Hanoi TIE trip provides authentic Humanities learning through visits
to historical sites, cultural landmarks and local communities. Students
examine how past events and geographical features continue to influence
societies today, while developing empathy, critical thinking and global
perspectives through direct observation and interaction among students.</p>

<h4><u>Shanghai–Wuxi, China: Cultural Immersion with Science, Mother Tongue Language and ALP (STEM)</u></h4>
<p><strong><em>Learning Focus<br></em></strong><em>Connecting science, language and innovation to urban and technological development</em>
</p>
<p>Set within an innovation‑driven environment, the Shanghai–Wuxi TIE trip
allows students to explore real‑world applications of science and STEM
through school immersion. Students also gain insights into intelligent
manufacturing, urban development and cultural heritage, strengthening curiosity,
problem‑solving and communication skills.</p>

<h4><u>Developing Global and Future‑Ready Zhenghuarians</u></h4>
<p>Through the Trip for International Experience, students grow as <strong>confident, culturally aware and reflective learners</strong>.
By integrating disciplinary learning with authentic overseas exposure,
TIE nurtures curiosity, resilience and respect for diversity — qualities
essential for students to thrive in a connected world.</p>
